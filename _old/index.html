Better government through innovation.

A growing number of Federal employees are using new approaches to solve problems, improve the core processes of government, and foster innovation to create a better government.

However, adoption of these approaches is still low relative to their potential. Many Federal employees do not know that these approaches exist or lack the support needed to use them effectively. This Toolkit captures these approaches and tells the stories of innovation happening within government. the Below are a few questions that this Toolkit helps answer:

When government works better, lives are improved and civic confidence grows. In order for government to work better, the practices of government must continually improve and evolve. In other words, government must continually innovate. Rapid social and technological changes, and the interconnectedness of the problems the world faces make innovation essential. Addressing unique challenges and opportunities of 21st century, like the governance of emerging technologies and increased global interdependence, requires innovative ways of solving these challenges. Three specific revolutions are occurring in parallel, which is driving forward innovation in the public sector: data, digital, and design.

Proven practices and actionable steps

The Better Government Toolkit captures the proven practices and actionable items that make a better government through innovation happen. It fosters the awareness, adaptation, and adoption of a core set of innovative principles to solve problems and promote a culture of experimentation both within the government and beyond.

Join the Better Government Movement

The Better Government Movement was officially launched with a series of human-centered design workshops in March 2017. Throughout 2017 the Community of Practice will collectively build a toolkit and create strategies to amplify and evangelize innovation across the federal government.

Innovation.gov and the Better Government Movement is a crowdsourced, open source movement brought to you by the General Services Administration, and built by hundreds of innovators across the Federal government.
